Agent Center · Highspot's agentic AI platform Highspot · 2025
01

The context

Agent Center brings specialist AI agents to sales enablement: strategy, GTM, and revenue workflows through conversational AI.

I focused on preventing design drift as scope grew: every new agent and capability needed to feel like one product, not eight.

That meant strategy and systems in parallel: principles for the whole platform, plus reusable patterns so teams could ship fast without fragmenting the experience.

The aim: a purposeful AI product with a clear domain identity, not a generic model bolted onto existing UI.

02

The design challenges

Two tensions drove most decisions.

I Challenge

Futuristic vision, shared foundation

Agent Center had to feel forward-looking while still evolving from Highspot's existing system: not a visual break, a coherent next step.

II Challenge

Flexibility without fragmentation

Frameworks had to give each specialist team room to solve their problems without the experience splintering across eight agents for the same user.

03

What I built

Design leadership

I established the language, interaction principles, and capability frameworks that gave Agent Center coherence and scale, architected to compound over time.

Framework 01

Intent recognition

How each agent maps signals to context-aware responses, the base for how all agents interpret intent across multi-turn flows.

Framework 02

Response

Patterns and structures for a consistent voice so switching agents feels like different experts, not different products.

Framework 03

Convergence

Human-in-the-loop handoffs for collaborative creation, the reusable spine behind live creation scenarios.

With visual design and foundations, I extended Polar UI with an agentic layer: libraries and interaction standards built for agent experiences, not a from-scratch system.
